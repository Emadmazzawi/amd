# AMD Developer Hackathon Guide

Based on the shortcut to the AMD Developer Hackathon on lablab.ai, here is a breakdown of what you need to do and how to participate successfully.

## What You Need to Do

Your goal is to build an AI agent or a high-performance AI application using AMD GPUs in the cloud. You can work solo or as part of a team to turn your ideas into a real AI system. 

### Choose a Track
You should align your project with one (or more) of the following tracks:

1. **Track 1: AI Agents & Agentic Workflows (Beginner Friendly)**
   * **Objective:** Build sophisticated AI systems that automate workflows, coordinate agents, or assist users in complex tasks.
   * **Tech Stack:** LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGen, connecting to open-source models (Llama, DeepSeek, Mistral, Qwen).

2. **Track 2: Fine-Tuning on AMD GPUs (Advanced)**
   * **Objective:** Fine-tune open-source models for domain specialization (Healthcare, Finance, Legal, Code).
   * **Tech Stack:** ROCm, PyTorch, Hugging Face Optimum-AMD, vLLM.

3. **Track 3: Vision & Multimodal AI**
   * **Objective:** Build applications that process images, video, or audio using massive memory bandwidth.
   * **Tech Stack:** Multimodal models like Llama 3.2 Vision, Qwen-VL optimized for ROCm.

4. **Extra Challenge: Build in Public**
   * Share at least 2 technical updates on X or LinkedIn.
   * Provide feedback about building with ROCm or AMD Developer Cloud.
   * Open-source your project or publish a technical walkthrough.

## How to Do It

### 1. Get Access & Compute
* **Sign up for the AMD AI Developer Program:** This is required to unlock **$100 in AMD Developer Cloud credits**.
* **Join the Hackathon:** Enroll on the [lablab.ai hackathon page](https://lablab.ai/ai-hackathons/amd-developer).
* **Join the Discord:** Join the lablab.ai Discord server to find teammates, get support, and stay updated.

### 2. Set Up Your Environment
You don't need any local hardware. All development will happen in the cloud.
* **AMD Developer Cloud:** Use your credits to spin up powerful GPU environments (like AMD Instinct MI300X) in minutes.
* **ROCm (Radeon Open Compute):** Utilize AMD’s open-source software platform to run PyTorch, TensorFlow, and your AI/ML workloads.
* **Hugging Face Hub:** You can pull models from the Hugging Face Hub, build/fine-tune them using AMD Cloud, and then deploy your final project back to Hugging Face as a Space. (Join the event's Hugging Face organization).

### 3. Build & Prepare Your Submission
Once you have built your prototype, you will need to prepare the following for your final submission:
* **Basic Info:** Project Title, Short/Long Descriptions, and Technology Tags.
* **Media:** A Cover Image, a Video Presentation showcasing your app, and a Slide Presentation.
* **Code & Demo:** A public GitHub Repository, your Application URL (e.g., hosted on Hugging Face Spaces), and the Demo platform.

### Summary
1. Sign up for AMD Developer Program.
2. Claim your $100 cloud credits.
3. Pick a track and spin up your cloud GPUs.
4. Build your AI Agent or App using ROCm/Hugging Face.
5. Record a demo and submit your code before the deadline.
